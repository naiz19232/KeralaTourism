Discover Kerala

Trivandrum

Trivandrum, officially known as Thiruvananthapuram, is the capital city of the Indian state of Kerala. It is a vibrant and culturally rich city with a lot to offer.

Kollam

Kollam, also known as Quilon, is famous for its backwaters, Ashtamudi Lake, and historical significance. It's a gateway to the southern backwater destinations in Kerala.

Pathanamthitta

Pathanamthitta is known for its lush green landscapes and pilgrimage sites, including Sabarimala, a major Hindu pilgrimage destination.

Alappuzha

Often called the "Venice of the East," Alappuzha is famous for its backwaters, houseboats, and the annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race.

Kottayam

Kottayam is known for its rubber plantations, beautiful landscapes, and cultural heritage. It's also the center of the Syrian Christian community in Kerala.

Idukki

Idukki is famous for its stunning hill stations, wildlife sanctuaries, and the Idukki Dam, one of the largest arch dams in Asia.

Ernakulam

Ernakulam, or Cochin, is a major commercial and industrial hub. It's known for its diverse culture, Fort Kochi, and the Chinese fishing nets.

Thrissur

Thrissur is often referred to as the "Cultural Capital of Kerala" and is famous for its Thrissur Pooram festival, Vadakkunnathan Temple, and rich cultural traditions.

Palakkad

Palakkad is known for its scenic beauty, historical forts, and lush paddy fields. The Silent Valley National Park is a notable attraction.

Malappuram

Malappuram is known for its educational institutions and historic sites, including the Kottakkal Arya Vaidya Sala, a renowned Ayurvedic treatment center.

Kozhikode

Kozhikode is known for its rich history, culture, and beautiful beaches. It's also a significant trade and commerce center.

Wayanad

Wayanad is a popular hill station known for its lush forests, wildlife sanctuaries, and tranquil natural beauty. It's an excellent destination for eco-tourism.

Kannur

Kannur is known for its historic forts, pristine beaches, and Theyyam rituals, which are an essential part of its culture.

Kasargode

Kasaragod is the northernmost district of Kerala and is famous for its coir and handloom industries, as well as its unique culture influenced by Karnataka and Goa.
